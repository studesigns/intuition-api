## Document 1: Global Entertainment & Client Relations Policy

**Filename**: `Global_Entertainment_Client_Relations_Policy.txt`

---

### ZENITH CORPORATION
### Global Entertainment & Client Relations Policy
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2025
**Policy Number**: ZEN-CLP-2025-01
**Classification**: GLOBAL - APPLIES TO ALL REGIONS

---

#### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy establishes standards for employee engagement with clients in social and entertainment settings. All Zenith employees are authorized to conduct client entertainment within the parameters established by this policy. This policy applies to all geographic regions worldwide unless superseded by regional addendums.

---

#### 1. SCOPE AND APPLICABILITY

**Section 1.1 Geographic Scope**: GLOBAL
This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and representatives of Zenith Corporation worldwide, including:
- North America (USA, Canada, Mexico)
- Europe (EU and non-EU countries)
- Asia-Pacific Region
- Middle East
- Africa
- All other geographies

**Section 1.2 Authorization Level**: Standard employee approval authority
- Employees may approve entertainment activities up to USD 500 per event
- Managers may approve up to USD 1,500 per event
- Director approval required for USD 1,500-5,000
- VP approval required for USD 5,000+

---

#### 2. PERMITTED ENTERTAINMENT ACTIVITIES

The following entertainment activities are explicitly PERMITTED under this global policy:

**Section 2.1 Dining & Beverages**
- Restaurant meals (any cuisine type)
- Cocktail bars and lounges
- Wine and spirits tastings
- Coffee and tea venues
- Hotel dining experiences

**Section 2.2 Sports & Recreation**
- Golf outings
- Tennis facilities
- Basketball courts
- Swimming facilities
- Ski resorts
- Water sports (boating, sailing)

**Section 2.3 Cultural Activities**
- Theater and performing arts
- Museums and galleries
- Movie theaters
- Concert venues
- Historical site tours
- Art festivals

**Section 2.4 Business Meals**
- Breakfast meetings
- Lunch meetings
- Dinner meetings
- Team lunches
- Client appreciation events

---

#### 3. RESTRICTED ACTIVITIES (REQUIRES SPECIAL APPROVAL)

The following activities are NOT automatically approved and require explicit manager/director approval:

**Section 3.1 High-Cost Events**
- Events exceeding USD 500 per person require manager pre-approval
- Events exceeding USD 1,500 per person require VP approval
- Luxury resort entertainment requires budget approval

**Section 3.2 Overnight Activities**
- Multi-day entertainment events
- Resort stays beyond standard business travel
- Adventure tourism activities

---

#### 4. EXPENSES AND REIMBURSEMENT

**Section 4.1 Reimbursable Expenses**
- Meal costs: 100% reimbursable per policy limits
- Tickets and entrance fees: 100% reimbursable
- Transportation to entertainment: 100% reimbursable
- Gratuities: 20% of pre-tax total (standard rates)

**Section 4.2 Non-Reimbursable Expenses**
- Personal alcohol consumption beyond business meal context
- Spousal or family member entertainment
- Activities that violate this policy or regional addendums

---

#### 5. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

**Section 5.1 Required Documentation**
All entertainment expenses must include:
- Date of activity
- Client name and organization
- Business purpose
- Amount spent
- Attendees (names and roles)
- Receipt or invoice

---

#### 6. COMPLIANCE AND AUDIT

This policy is subject to:
- Quarterly audit review
- Annual employee training
- Finance department verification of expenses
- Management certification of business purpose

---

#### 7. VIOLATIONS AND ENFORCEMENT

**Section 7.1 Enforcement**
- First violation: Written warning
- Second violation: Mandatory retraining
- Third violation: Disciplinary action up to termination

---

**Policy Approval**:
- Global Chief Compliance Officer: Sarah Mitchell
- Global CFO: Robert Chen
- CEO Signature: Margaret Williams

**Last Revised**: January 1, 2025
**Next Review Date**: January 1, 2026

## Document 2: Asia-Pacific High-Risk Activities Regional Addendum

**Filename**: `Regional_Addendum_APAC_High_Risk_Activities.txt`

---

### ZENITH CORPORATION
### Asia-Pacific Region: Prohibited High-Risk Entertainment Activities
**Regional Addendum to Global Entertainment Policy**
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2025
**Policy Number**: ZEN-CLP-APAC-2025-01
**Geographic Scope**: ASIA-PACIFIC REGION ONLY

---

#### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This addendum modifies the Global Entertainment & Client Relations Policy specifically for the Asia-Pacific region. Due to regulatory, cultural, and business-specific considerations in APAC, certain entertainment activities are PROHIBITED in this region, even though they may be permitted globally.

**CRITICAL**: This addendum applies ONLY to the following APAC countries:
- China (PRC)
- Japan
- South Korea
- Taiwan
- Vietnam
- Indonesia
- Thailand
- Malaysia
- Philippines
- Singapore

**This addendum does NOT apply to**: Europe, North America, Middle East, Africa, or any other non-APAC regions.

---

#### 1. PROHIBITED ENTERTAINMENT ACTIVITIES IN APAC REGION

**Section 1.1 Explicitly Prohibited Activities**

The following entertainment activities are STRICTLY PROHIBITED in the Asia-Pacific region:

**1.1.1 Karaoke Venues**
- Private karaoke bars
- Karaoke lounges
- Entertainment complexes featuring karaoke
- Establishment of any type where karaoke is primary activity

**Reason for Prohibition**: APAC regulatory environment. Certain karaoke establishments in the region operate under questionable legal structures and may involve improper inducements. Zenith employees must avoid any appearance of impropriety.

**Prohibited in**: China, Japan, South Korea, Vietnam, Indonesia, Thailand, Malaysia
**NOT Prohibited in**: Europe, Americas, Middle East, Africa

---

**1.1.2 Nightclub Entertainment (Late-Night)**
- Nightclubs and dance clubs
- Discotheques with adult entertainment
- Late-night entertainment venues (operating after 11 PM as primary business)

**Reason for Prohibition**: Regulatory risk and business conduct standards in APAC.

**Prohibited in**: China, Japan, South Korea, Vietnam, Indonesia, Thailand
**NOT Prohibited in**: Europe, Americas, Middle East, Africa

---

**1.1.3 Hostess Bars and Adult Entertainment Establishments**
- Any venue with hostess services
- Adult entertainment venues
- Escort service coordination
- Any activity involving hired companions for entertainment

**Reason for Prohibition**: Anti-bribery and improper inducement policies. APAC regulatory environment has stricter requirements around this.

**Prohibited in**: All APAC countries listed above
**NOT Prohibited in**: Other regions (though management approval recommended)

---

**1.1.4 Gambling Facilities**
- Casinos
- Gambling establishments
- Gaming venues
- Betting parlors

**Reason for Prohibition**: Compliance with FCPA and regional anti-corruption standards.

**Prohibited in**: China, Vietnam, Indonesia, Malaysia
**NOT Prohibited in**: Japan (select licensed establishments permitted with VP approval), Singapore (highly regulated)

---

#### 2. PERMITTED ACTIVITIES IN APAC REGION

The following activities ARE permitted in APAC (per Global Policy):
- Fine dining restaurants
- Golf outings (courses approved by Finance)
- Museum visits and cultural events
- Theater and performing arts
- Hotel business dining
- Team activities (sports, recreational facilities)
- Business conferences and seminars

---

#### 3. AUTHORITY AND APPROVAL CHANGES FOR APAC

**Section 3.1 Approval Requirements for APAC**

Any entertainment activity costing over USD 300 in APAC requires VP approval (vs. USD 500 globally).

**Reason**: Heightened compliance risk in region requires additional oversight.

---

#### 4. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS (APAC-SPECIFIC)

**Section 4.1 Enhanced Documentation**

All APAC entertainment expenses must include:
- Date, time, and location of activity
- Client name, organization, and title
- Business purpose (specific details required)
- Amount spent (in USD and local currency)
- Complete list of attendees with titles
- Signed attestation by employee and manager
- Finance department pre-approval (all expenses, no exceptions)

**Section 4.2 Prohibited Documentation Statements**

Employees are strictly forbidden from:
- Describing prohibited activities as "alternative entertainment"
- Listing karaoke as "cultural activity"
- Describing nightclubs as "networking events"
- Mischaracterizing activity location or nature

---

#### 5. VIOLATIONS IN APAC REGION

**Section 5.1 APAC-Specific Enforcement**

Violations of this APAC addendum carry enhanced penalties:
- First violation: Mandatory retraining + written warning + loss of entertainment authority
- Second violation: Suspension of business entertainment privileges + mandatory investigation
- Third violation: Disciplinary action up to immediate termination

**Reason**: Heightened compliance risk in region requires strict enforcement.

---

#### 6. BUSINESS JUDGMENT CLAUSE

**Section 6.1 When in Doubt**

If an activity's compliance status is unclear in APAC:
- Err on the side of caution
- Obtain VP approval before proceeding
- Document the business purpose in detail
- Finance must pre-approve all ambiguous activities

---

#### 7. REGIONAL EXEMPTIONS

**Section 7.1 Limited Exemptions**

The following activities MAY be permitted in select APAC countries with explicit VP pre-approval:
- Gambling in Japan (government-licensed facilities only)
- Late-night (post-11pm) dining in Singapore (permitted, nightclubs still prohibited)
- Golf outings in China (courses must be pre-approved by Regional Compliance)

**All exemptions require**:
- Written VP approval
- Finance certification
- Client relationship documentation
- Compliance review

---

#### 8. POLICY COORDINATION

**Section 8.1 Precedence**

This APAC addendum supersedes the Global Policy where more restrictive.
- Global Policy permits activity → APAC addendum prohibits → **Activity is PROHIBITED in APAC**
- Global Policy permits activity → APAC addendum silent → **Activity is PERMITTED in APAC**

**Example**:
- Karaoke: Global permits (not mentioned), but APAC explicitly prohibits → **PROHIBITED**
- Karaoke in Germany: Global permits, APAC not applicable (Germany not in scope) → **PERMITTED**

## Document 3: Global Business Travel & Entertainment Expenses Policy

**Filename**: `Global_Business_Travel_Entertainment_Expenses_Policy.txt`

### ZENITH CORPORATION
### Global Business Travel & Entertainment Expenses Policy
**Policy Number**: ZEN-TRAVEL-2025-01
**Classification**: GLOBAL - APPLIES TO ALL REGIONS
**Effective Date**: January 1, 2025

---

#### EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This policy governs business travel and entertainment expenses for all Zenith Corporation employees worldwide. This policy works in conjunction with the Global Entertainment & Client Relations Policy and regional addendums.

---

#### 1. ENTERTAINMENT EXPENSE CATEGORIES

**Section 1.1 Categories**

Entertainment expenses are categorized as:
- **Client Entertainment**: Meals and activities with external clients
- **Team Building**: Internal employee activities
- **Business Development**: Prospecting and networking
- **Relationship Maintenance**: Ongoing client engagement

---

#### 2. APPROVAL MATRIX

**Section 2.1 Global Approval Requirements**

| Amount | Approval Required | Who Approves | Exception |
|--------|-----------------|--------------|-----------|
| Under USD 100 | None | N/A | None |
| USD 100-500 | Manager | Direct Manager | Same-day expense reporting |
| USD 500-1,500 | Director | Department Director | Annual limit: USD 10,000 |
| USD 1,500-5,000 | VP | Vice President | Annual limit: USD 25,000 |
| Over USD 5,000 | C-Suite | CFO or CEO | Annual limit: USD 50,000 |

**Section 2.2 APAC Region Exception**

In Asia-Pacific region, the following modifications apply:
- Under USD 300 requires Manager approval (vs. no approval globally)
- USD 300-1,000 requires VP approval (vs. Manager globally)
- All APAC expenses require Finance pre-approval regardless of amount

---

#### 3. NON-REIMBURSABLE ITEMS

**Section 3.1 Prohibited Expenses**

The following are never reimbursable:
- Personal entertainment (movies, concerts for self only)
- Alcohol for personal consumption (only in business meal context)
- Activities that violate regional addendums
- Expenses at prohibited venues
- Spousal or family member entertainment
- Gambling losses

---

#### 4. COMPLIANCE WITH ANTI-CORRUPTION LAWS

**Section 4.1 FCPA Compliance**

All entertainment must comply with:
- Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA)
- UK Bribery Act
- Local anti-corruption laws
- Zenith Code of Conduct

**Section 4.2 Inducement Prohibition**

Entertainment expenses must NOT:
- Induce a customer to purchase products/services
- Be conditioned on a sale or contract
- Violate local customs or norms
- Create appearance of impropriety

---

#### 5. REGIONAL POLICY INTEGRATION

**Section 5.1 Regional Addendums**

Where regional addendums exist, they take precedence:
- Asia-Pacific addendum supersedes this policy on prohibited activities
- Regional restrictions are MORE restrictive than global policy
- Employees must follow most restrictive applicable policy

**Example**:
- Karaoke is not mentioned in this policy (implicitly permitted globally)
- APAC addendum explicitly prohibits karaoke in APAC
- Therefore: Karaoke is permitted globally but PROHIBITED in APAC

---

#### 6. DOCUMENTATION AND RECEIPT REQUIREMENTS

**Section 6.1 Required Documentation**

All expenses must include:
- Receipt or invoice
- Business purpose (2-3 sentences minimum)
- Client name and organization
- Date and location of entertainment
- All attendees (names and titles)
- Employee name and department

**Section 6.2 Missing Receipt Policy**

- Under USD 50: No receipt required if documented
- USD 50-250: Receipt required unless documented as lost
- Over USD 250: Receipt always required, no exceptions

---

#### 7. TRAINING AND CERTIFICATION

**Section 7.1 Annual Requirements**

All managers and above must:
- Complete annual anti-corruption training
- Certify understanding of entertainment policy
- Complete FCPA training
- Review regional addendums applicable to region

---

#### 8. AUDIT AND ENFORCEMENT

**Section 8.1 Regular Audits**

Entertainment expenses are subject to:
- Monthly finance review
- Quarterly audit sampling
- Annual compliance certification
- Regular training updates

**Section 8.2 Violations**

- First violation: Written warning
- Second violation: Training + probation
- Third violation: Up to termination
